§ Brief

The ask.

QMFS needed bodywork that was lighter, stiffer, and compliant with IMechE Formula Student structural and scrutineering rules — without blowing the team budget. Previous years ran wet-layup glass composites; the target was to move the full aerodynamic package to carbon fibre via resin infusion while keeping costs defensible to sponsors and judges.

I led the programme end-to-end across two seasons: material trade studies, FEA sign-off, the CBOM, sponsor negotiation, and the infusion itself — then handed it over to a mentored junior for the third season.

§ Approach

Wet-layup vs. resin infusion.

I ran a formal trade study comparing the two manufacturing routes against the car's performance envelope. Wet-layup was familiar and cheap but inconsistent on fibre-to-resin ratio; infusion demanded tighter process control but promised a lower, repeatable resin fraction.

Using CES EduPack for material ranking and ANSYS FEA to stress-check candidate laminate schedules, I specified a 3-ply twill schedule that met the structural margin against downforce loads while hitting the mass target. The report was presented to Formula 1 engineers serving as competition judges.
