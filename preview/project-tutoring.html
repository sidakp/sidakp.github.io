§ Practice

Small roster, long arcs.

I run my own practice of 20+ students, most of whom stay with me across multiple years and exam series. Lessons are one-to-one, planned around each student's actual weaknesses rather than a blanket curriculum, with structured written reports for parents.

The work is a counterweight to the engineering — patient, iterative, and measured in outcomes that show up slowly. It also keeps my own mathematical fundamentals honest.

§ Outcomes

What changed.

Selective entry: students placed into Kent grammar schools via the 11+, including cases where prior mock results made entry unlikely.

Failure reversal: multiple students brought from prior exam failures back to target grades within a single academic year.

Four-grade swings: one student's GCSE maths and science performance lifted by four full grades through a structured catch-up programme.

SEND work: tailored lesson plans for students with long-term medical conditions, including extended catch-up for periods of absence.

Teaching is the fastest way to find out which parts of a subject you only think you understand.
