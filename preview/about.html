About

Engineer, tutor, quietly building.

I'm an MEng (Hons) Aerospace Engineering student at Queen Mary University of London, based in Mile End. My interests sit at the intersection of composites manufacturing, CFD/FEA, and orbital mechanics — things that fly, and things that orbit.

For the past two seasons I've been the Senior Composites Design Engineer at Queen Mary Formula Student, leading the programme that moved the car's aerodynamic bodywork from wet-layup glass to resin-infused carbon. Outside the team I run an independent research project on active space-debris removal via J2 differential precession, and I have done for the past two years.

I also tutor. Three years of my own private practice covering 11+, GCSE, A-Level and IB maths and science — a patient counterweight to the engineering work.

Previously I interned in mechanical engineering at KiwiRail in New Zealand, reviewing CAD against maintenance standards across 200+ mainline locomotives.
