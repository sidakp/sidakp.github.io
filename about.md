    <p class="reveal">
      I'm an <strong>MEng (Hons) Aerospace Engineering</strong> student at Queen Mary
      University of London, based in Mile End. My interests sit at the intersection
      of <em class="a">composites manufacturing</em>, <em class="a">CFD/FEA</em>,
      and <em class="a">orbital mechanics</em> — things that fly, and things that orbit.
    </p>
    <p class="reveal delay-1">
      For the past two seasons I've been the Senior Composites Design Engineer at
      Queen Mary Formula Student, leading the programme that moved the car's aerodynamic
      bodywork from wet-layup glass to resin-infused carbon. Outside the team I run an
      independent research project on <strong>active space-debris removal</strong> via
      J2 differential precession.
    </p>
    <p class="reveal delay-2">
      I also tutor. Three years of my own private practice covering 11+, GCSE, A-Level
      and IB maths and science — a patient counterweight to the engineering work.
    </p>
    <p class="reveal delay-3">
      Previously I interned in mechanical engineering at KiwiRail in New Zealand,
      reviewing CAD against maintenance standards across 200+ mainline locomotives.
    </p>
